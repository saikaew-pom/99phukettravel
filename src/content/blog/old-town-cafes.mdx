Phuket Old Town is more than just a collection of colorful Sino-Portuguese shophouses; it's the beating heart of the island's burgeoning specialty coffee and dessert scene. While the Sunday Walking Street Market on Thalang Road draws the biggest crowds, the true charm of the Old Town lies in its sophisticated, often hidden, cafes.

Based on local favorites and insights from top island guides like **Phuket 101**, here are the absolute must-visit cafes in Phuket Old Town.

## 1. ROÔF Pudding & Café (Yaowarat Road)

Located in a masterfully renovated heritage shophouse, **ROÔF Pudding & Café** is a masterpiece of minimalist design. The standout feature is a large tree growing right in the center of the cafe, reaching toward a glass ceiling that floods the space with natural light.

*   **What to order:** Their signature **puddings** are legendary—silky, rich, and perfectly balanced. Pair it with a classic cold brew or a signature latte.
*   **Vibe:** Modern, Instagram-worthy, and surprisingly tranquil.

## 2. Torry’s Ice Cream (Soi Romanee)

No visit to the Old Town is complete without a stop at **Torry’s**. Located on the picturesque Soi Romanee—voted one of the most beautiful streets in the world—Torry’s specializes in premium ice cream that celebrates Phuket’s culinary heritage.

*   **What to order:** The **Bi Co Moi** (black glutinous rice with coconut milk) or the **O-Aew** ice cream, which uses the local refreshing jelly made from banana starch and fig seeds.
*   **Vibe:** Vintage luxury. The interior is decorated in a classic Peranakan style that feels like stepping back in time.

## 3. Campus Coffee Roaster (Krabi Road)

For the serious coffee aficionado, **Campus Coffee Roaster** is the gold standard. This isn't just a cafe; it's a dedicated roastery where the craft of coffee is taken to the next level.

*   **What to order:** Their **Nitro Cold Brew** is incredibly smooth, or ask for a **Drip Coffee** using their latest seasonal Thai beans.
*   **Vibe:** "Old School" library vibes. It's a popular spot for digital nomads and locals who appreciate a high-quality, focused brew.

## 4. Aungku Phuket (Thalang Road)

Positioned at the iconic intersection of Thalang Road and Soi Romanee, **Aungku Phuket** is a favorite for those who love a bold, floral aesthetic. The exterior is famous for its vibrant artificial flower displays and mirrored walls.

*   **What to order:** Their refreshing fruit-based teas and colorful signature drinks are perfect for cooling down after a walk through the Sunday market.
*   **Vibe:** Vibrant and high-energy—ideal for people-watching and quick refreshment.

## 5. Ryn – Authentic Tea & Slow Drop Coffee

If you need a break from the hustle, **Ryn** is your sanctuary. This chic, minimalist space is dedicated to the art of "slow" drinks—specifically Japanese matcha and meticulous drip coffee.

*   **What to order:** The **Premium Matcha** is whisked to perfection, or try a slow-drip coffee for a cleaner, more nuanced caffeine hit.
*   **Vibe:** Zen-like and quiet. A perfect escape for those who value quality over quantity.

## Pro Tips for Cafe Hopping in Old Town

1.  **Avoid the Heat:** Visit between 10:00 AM and 12:00 PM for the best lighting and fewer crowds.
2.  **Sunday Strategy:** If you visit during the Sunday Walking Street Market, expect these cafes to be very busy. Try visiting on a Tuesday or Wednesday for a much more relaxed experience.
3.  **Explore the Alleys:** Some of the best spots aren't on Thalang Road. Take a detour down Soi Romanee, Yaowarat Road, or Dibuk Road to find the newest "specialty" additions to the scene.

Phuket Old Town’s cafe culture is a perfect reflection of the island itself: a beautiful blend of deep-rooted history and modern, creative energy. Whether you're a coffee nerd or a dessert lover, there's a shophouse waiting for you.
